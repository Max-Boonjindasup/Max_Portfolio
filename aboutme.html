I graduated from the University of California, San Diego in 2018 with a double major in biochemistry and cognitive science (specialized in machine learning). Following my interests in biological systems, I joined Siemens, Amgen, and Cedars-Sinai to expand upon my research training.

During my time at these companies, I noticed a striking disparity between tech and non-tech companies where many valuable skills and tools failed to translate over. Wanting to bridge this gap, I created an automated system that extracted data from machines, analyzed and documented the data, and produced comprehensive statistical dashboards. I was thrilled to share my project with the company, and I was later given the opportunity to lead a team to develop it further.

This experience solidified my interest in the intersection of healthcare and technology, and I knew that I wanted to use my problem-solving research skills to make a positive impact on the world. I believe that data science can be used to improve healthcare and address critical challenges such as workforce shortages and rising costs. Now, I am excited to continue my journey of discovering and developing more machine learning models that can uncover meaningful and impactful solutions through data.
